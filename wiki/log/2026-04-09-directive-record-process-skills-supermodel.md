# Record the Process — Skills, Super-Model, Preach by Example

## Summary

The entire model-building process needs to be recorded, turned into skills, and the super-model must evolve naturally as the second brain grows. Models and standards must preach by example — they must themselves respect what they instaurate.

## Operator Directive (verbatim)

> have we recorded this whole process ? and have we made a skill or even skills of it all. parent chilren relationship, lets remember it was about not only models but a supermodel, so it should become natural that the second-brain evolve and its super-model with it too. and the new added models and standards document with proper examples too and that prove that they respect what they are themselves meant to instaure. preach by example. a good model. and even possibly the layers before that before we could get to them.

## Interpretation

Multiple requirements:

1. **Record the process** — the journey of building models (from empty → reading lists → real definitions) should itself be documented. Not just the result — the process.

2. **Skills from the process** — the model-building workflow should become skills that can be invoked: /build-model, /review-model, /set-standards. So the second brain naturally evolves its models.

3. **Super-model relationship** — the Methodology Framework is the super-model. It CONTAINS the 14 sub-models. This parent-child relationship must be explicit and navigable. When a new model is added, the super-model evolves.

4. **Preach by example** — every model page and standards page must ITSELF follow the standards it defines. The LLM Wiki model page must pass its own quality gates. The standards page must demonstrate its own anti-patterns avoidance. Self-referential integrity.

5. **The layers before the models** — before we could build models, we needed: foundation (maturity + layer on all pages), then entry points, then model definitions, then standards. This SFIF-like progression should be documented as a lesson or pattern.

6. **Natural evolution** — the second brain should have built-in mechanisms to grow new models, evolve existing ones, and keep the super-model current. Not manual — natural.
