## What This Names

### The 10 verbs of the knowledge project (operator's definition)

1. **Aggregate** — pull from sisters, external sources
2. **Process** — ingest, synthesize
3. **Evaluate** — score, validate, review
4. **Learn** — extract lessons
5. **Integrate** — cross-reference, arc-link across projects
6. **Modelize** — promote to patterns, models, principles
7. **Validate** — evidence-gate
8. **Standardize** — per-type standards, templates
9. **Teach** — structure, surface, index
10. **Offer** — view, search, guidance (gateway ops)

### The virus metaphor

The second brain spreads OUTWARD (config, standards, methodology → sisters) AND pulls INWARD (lessons, patterns from sister operations). Sister projects are infected with flexible AI & Wiki configurations; knowledge flows back.

### The gap identified

- Current methodology / gateway / goldilocks / SDLC is app-project-shaped.
- `feature-development` / `document→design→scaffold→implement→test` is the dominant stage grammar.
- `gateway what-do-i-need` routes to task-picking, not fresh-agent orientation.
- `gateway flow` walks operational 8 steps (detect→declare→select→stage→produce→track→feedback).
- For a knowledge project (the brain itself, being consumed from inside by a fresh agent), this is wrong shape.
- Even for app projects, what exists is a scaffold — not fully evolved per each project's operational reality.

## The Standing Rule This Establishes

**Context-aware gateway routing is now a first-class requirement.** The gateway must recognize:
- WHO is invoking (fresh agent in brain / task-bound in sister / external consumer / harness)
- WHAT phase (orientation needed / task-routing needed / aggregation cycle / evolution cycle)
- WHICH track (operational app-dev / orientation / knowledge-evolution)

It cannot answer every invocation with "pick a task model."
