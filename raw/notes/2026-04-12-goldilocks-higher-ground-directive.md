> "we are going to want to attain and offer the goldilocks adapted to the different projects and situations and methodologies."
> "'Goldilocks' describes a situation, product, or condition that is optimal—neither too extreme nor too mild—often referred to as 'just right'"

### The Self-Identification Questions

> "AM I a system? Am I a harness? V2? V3? Am I just a solo agent session? What is my domain and my type of project? What make this works? Where is all the magic? What is the intelligence, the structure and the way? What are the ways and level of adhering / using it as needed?"

## Requirements Extracted

1. **Goldilocks principle** — every project gets "just right" process. Not too much (over-process kills POC), not too little (under-process kills production). The framework ADAPTS to context.

2. **Self-identification protocol** — any agent or system connecting to the second brain should be able to answer: What am I? (system/harness/solo). What version? (v1/v2/v3). What domain? What project type? What makes this work? Where is the intelligence? What level of adherence?

3. **Dual-scope tools** — wiki tools work on BOTH the second brain (information hub) AND project-internal wikis. One toolset, two contexts. Projects call the second-brain tools to query methodology, standards, chains — then apply them to their own wiki.

4. **Continuous improvement** — not "build and ship" but "build, review, improve, review, improve." Global sweep of everything. Human confirmations at each level.

5. **Global standards adherence** — CloudEvents, OpenAPI, SFIF, Design Patterns, Domain-Driven Design, Onion Architecture, SRP, OOP, documented custom patterns. The wiki's own structure should adhere to recognized patterns, not invent everything from scratch.

6. **Not just OpenArms** — the sister project knowledge is ONE input. The higher ground is the framework itself, constantly evolving, integrating from all sources.
